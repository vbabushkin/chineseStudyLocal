知情同意书

我们邀请您参加这个关于双重文化身份的研究。研究者为纽约大学阿布扎比分校 Kartik Sreenivasan 博士以及合作者 Nela Noll 。请认真阅读下面的研究简介。如果您选择参加本研究，请勾选页面底部的“是的，我接受”选项。如果您不想参加，请勾选“不，我拒绝”选项。 谢谢！

目的:本研究旨在检验双重文化身份认同以及对于来自这些文化的刺激的反应。

程序:作为被试，您的任务是填写三个简单的问卷，之后在一些问题中写 4-6 个句子作为回答，最后看一些图片并对出现在图片上的圆点进行按键反应。实验需要大约60分钟完成。

自愿参加:参加本研究完全基于自愿。您可以选择完成或不完成本研究。您可以拒绝参加或者在任何时候退出而不会受到惩罚。在问卷部分，您有权跳过或不回答任何您不想回答的问题。

奖励:您参加本实验将获得每小时6美元的报酬。

研究利益:本实验可以帮助研究者了解双重文化身份认同。尽管您不会获得直接利益，但是研究结果能够帮助我们更好的理解人们是如何形成并保持双重文化身份认同的。

风险及不便:除了花费时间，参加实验本身不会带来其他不便或风险。作为研究内容的一部分，我们可能会问您一些敏感话题，例如死亡或疼痛。

保密:任何经由本研究获得的有关您身份的信息将会被严格保密，依照法律只会在您同意后披露。研究产生的信息将以身份编码保存在电脑中。此编码与您身份之间的关联信息将保存在安全的地点。整体的研究结果可能会发表，但是您的名字和身份信息不会在任何出版物中出现。您的研究记录会以电子文件形式无期限保存在安全的地点。这些文件只有 Kartik Sreenivasan 博士以及他的合作者 Nela Noll 可以获取。

联系方式:本研究由纽约大学阿布扎比分校的 Kartik Sreenivasan 博士负责 (kks4@nyu.edu) 。如果您有任何关于本研究的问题，请联系合作研究者 Nela Noll (nela.noll@nyu.edu) 。本研究已被纽约大学阿布扎比分校人类被试研究伦理审查委员会审查并批准。如果您有涉及被试权利的问题，请联系纽约大学阿布扎比分校研究伦理审查委员会 nyuad@nyu.edu 或 +971 2-628-4313 。

Consent

You are invited to take part in a study about bicultural identities. This study is conducted by Dr. Kartik Sreenivasan and his associate Nela Noll from New York University Abu Dhabi (NYUAD).Below is a brief description of what you can expect if you choose to participate in this study. Please read this form carefully. After reading the form, if you consent to participate, please press the button "Yes I agree"; at the bottom of the page. Thank you!

Purpose: The goal of this project is to examine bicultural identities and their reactions to stimuli from these cultures.

Procedure: Your task as a participant is to fill out three simple surveys,write 4-6 sentences in response to a prompt, and view several images and react to a dot that will appear on the images with a button press. This should take around 60minutes of your time. No other action is required on your part.

Volunteer participation:Your participation to this study is solely on a voluntary basis. It is up to you whether or not to complete the study (participation in this study is voluntary). You may refuse to participate or withdraw at any time without penalty. For questionnaires, youhave the right to skip or not answer any questions you prefer not to answer.

Incentives: You will receive an hourly rate of US $6 for taking part in this study.

Benefits: The research is designed to help investigators learn more about bicultural identities. Although you will receive no direct benefits, this research may help us better understand how people formand hold on tobicultural identities.

Risks and inconveniences:Beside the time investment, no inconveniences or risks are associated with your participation to this study by or from itself. As part of the study, we may ask you to reflect on some sensitive topics, such as death or pain.

Confidentiality:Any information that is obtained in connection with this study and that can be identified with you will remain confidential and will be disclosed only with your permission or as required by law. Information produced from the study will be stored in computer files with an identifying code. The link between this code and your identity will be kept in a secure location. The overall results of these studies may be published but your name will not be used and your identity will not be revealed anywhere in these publications. Your research records will be kept on file electronically in a secure location, and will be saved indefinitely. These files will only be accessible to Dr. Sreenivasan or his Co-Investigator, Nela Noll.

Contact information: This project is led by Dr. Kartik Sreenivasan at NYUAD (kks4@nyu.edu).If you have any questions or concerns pertaining to the research, please communicate with co-investigatorNela Noll (nela.noll@nyu.edu). This research has been reviewed and approved according to New York University Abu DhabiIRB procedures for research involving human subjects.If you have questions regarding your rights as research participant, please communicate with the Institutional Review Board of NYUAD at IRBnyuad@nyu.edu or +971 2-628-4313.

Debriefing

Thank you very much for taking part in our study. In the following, you can read more about the purpose of this research as well as the specific hypotheses investigated.

The purpose of this research was to examine the relationship between bicultural individual's salience of death-related thoughts and their reaction to cultural stimuli. During the first survey, we measured the extent to which you identify as bicultural. We then induced thoughts of either death or dental pain to affect the degree to which thoughts about your identity were relevant to you and how much you would hold on to them. Then, you saw a series of images that were related to both of your cultural identities. By measuring the time, it took you to respond to the dots, we were able to measure the degree to which you paid attention to each identity.

We hypothesized that participants who were induced with death-related thoughts and were made more salient of one of their identities through language would pay more attention to one of their cultural identities. These findings will expand our understanding of how people form and hold on to cultural identities.
